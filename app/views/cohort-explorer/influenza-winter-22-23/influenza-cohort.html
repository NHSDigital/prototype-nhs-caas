Summary

The Technology workstream within the NHS Digital Vaccination Deployment Programme (VDP) will send patient identifiable data to the National Immunisation Management Service (NIMS). This will allow for monitoring of flu vaccinations and call and recall during the 2022 to 2023 influenza season (1 September 2022 to 31 March 2023).

Cohorting as a Service (CaaS) will provide patient identifiable cohorts to VDP. This will help to identify citizens who are eligible for flu vaccinations.

CaaS will use criteria outlined in chapter 19 of the influenza Green Book to identify eligible and clinical at-risk citizens for flu vaccinations.

Clinical at-risk groups

You can find the Green Book rules and code list for each condition in the links below.

This list below is not exhaustive, and decisions should be based on clinical judgement.

The clinical at-risk groups within the Green Book are:

  • Chronic respiratory disease
  • Chronic heart disease and vascular disease
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Chronic liver disease
  • Chronic neurological disease
  • Clinically coded eligibility
  • Diabetes and adrenal insufficiency
  • Immunosuppression including asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen
  • Morbid obesity (class 3 obesity)
  • Pregnancy

Demographics

All people aged 6 months or older that are in the clinical risk groups shown in table 19.4, chapter 19 of the Green Book.
