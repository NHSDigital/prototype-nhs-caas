Immunosuppression including asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen

Rule logic

The information below shows the digital pathway rule logic for the conditions that have been identified for call and recall for the flu season winter 2022 to 2023.

These conditions are detailed in chapter 19 of the influenza Green Book.

    • Condition description

      Immunosuppression due to disease leading to immunosuppression, bone marrow transplant, HIV infection at all stages, multiple myeloma or genetic disorders affecting the immune system (e.g. IRAK-4, NEMO, complement disorder).

      It’s difficult to define at what level of immunosuppression a patient could be considered to be at a greater risk of the serious consequences of flu and should be offered a vaccination.
      This decision is best made on an individual basis and left to the patient’s clinician.

    • Data used

      GPES v4 and GDPPR

    • Conditions following a non-digital pathway

      All people over 6 months of age.

      People with immunosuppression due to haematological disease (including multiple myeloma), bone marrow transplant, HIV infection at all stages, Down's syndrome, neutropenia.
      Also, some genetic causes of immunosuppression (such as severe combined immunodeficiency) and acquired causes of immunosuppression.

    • Condition description

      Immunosuppression due to treatment leading to immunosuppression.

      This includes patients undergoing chemotherapy and individuals treated with or likely to be treated with systemic steroids for more than a month at a dose equivalent to prednisolone at 20mg or more per day (any age).
      For children under 20kg, a dose of 1mg or more per kg per day.

    • Data used

      GPES v4 and GDPPR

    • Ruleset

      All people over 6 months of age prescribed immunosuppressants by their GP (apart from oral steroids).

      Also, some people over 6 months of age on chemotherapy within the last 12 months.

    • Condition description

      Asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen.

      This also includes conditions such as homozygous sickle cell disease, hereditary spherocytosis, thalassemia major and coeliac syndrome that may lead to splenic dysfunction.

    • Data used

      GPES v4 and GDPPR

    • Ruleset

      All people with a splenectomy or splenic damage, sickle cell anaemia, thalasaemia, spherocytosis, coeliac syndrome and some other diseases that cause asplenia or splenic dysfunction.

Non-digital pathways

Data that is held within a GP clinical system does not hold a patients complete medical records. This means that a percentage of the patients identified within the clinical policy will not have been captured in the digital patient cohort.

If you believe you are entitled to an NHS flu vaccination and you have not been included in the digital cohort, you can visit the NHS website on the flu vaccine.

    • Condition description

      Immunosuppression due to disease leading to immunosuppression, bone marrow transplant, HIV infection at all stages, multiple myeloma or genetic disorders affecting the immune system (e.g. IRAK-4, NEMO, complement disorder).

      It’s difficult to define at what level of immunosuppression a patient could be considered to be at a greater risk of the serious consequences of flu and should be offered flu vaccination. This decision is best made on an individual basis and left to the patient’s clinician.

    • Conditions following a non-digital pathway

      NHS Digital clinicians have worked hard to include all conditions that may cause immunosuppression. As there is no clinically agreed list of these conditions, the code clusters may not be complete.

      People with immunocompromise due to disease, may not be included in the digital cohort unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT code to show eligibility for flu vaccination.

    • Condition description

      Immunosuppression due to treatment leading to immunosuppression.

      This includes patients undergoing chemotherapy and individuals treated with or likely to be treated with systemic steroids for more than a month at a dose equivalent to prednisolone at 20mg or more per day (any age).
      For children under 20kg, a dose of 1mg or more per kg per day.

    • Conditions following a non-digital pathway

      Data for hospital prescribed medication is not held centrally, neither is community systemic steroid prescribing data held centrally in a format whereby duration and dose of treatment can be ascertained.

      People on systemic steroids prescribed by their GP or hospital prescribed immunosuppressants, may not be included in the digital cohort unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT code to show eligibility for flu vaccination
      Patient data held in GP records does not generally identify people on chemotherapy.
      People who are immunosuppressed due to current or recent chemotherapy treatment, may not be included in the digital cohort unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT code to show eligibility for flu vaccination.

    • Condition description

      Asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen. This includes conditions such as homozygous sickle cell disease, hereditary spherocytosis, thalassemia major and coeliac syndrome that may lead to splenic dysfunction.

    • Conditions following a non-digital pathway

      NHS Digital clinicians have worked hard to include all conditions that may cause asplenia or splenic dysfunction. As there is no clinically agreed list of these conditions, the code clusters may not be complete.

      People with asplenia or splenic dysfunction due to diseases , may not be included in the digital cohort unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT code to show eligibility for flu vaccination.
