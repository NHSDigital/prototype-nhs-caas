Summary

NHS England commissioned Cohorting as a Service (CaaS) to identify people that were eligible for the autumn 2022 to 2023 COVID-19 booster vaccination.

To be eligible, they needed to be:

  • 6 months of age or older
  • in defined clinical risk groups shown in tables 3 and 4 of the Green Book, chapter 14a

CaaS developed specific rules based on the conditions in the Green Book and used medical data to identify people who met those rules.

The data collected helped to:

  • contact or remind people to receive their COVID autumn booster
  • focus protection on those at clinical risk of COVID-19
  • report and monitor on the 2022 to 2023 autumn booster campaign

Clinical at-risk groups

The links below provide detailed information on the clinical rules that determine who is eligible for the COVID-19 booster.

The rules apply to:

  • children aged 5 to 15 years within specific clinical at-risk groups
  • adults aged 16 years and over

The clinical at-risk groups are:

  • Asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic heart disease and vascular disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic kidney and liver disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic kidney disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic liver disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic neurological disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic respiratory disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Diabetes mellitus and other endocrine disorders (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Haematological disease (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Immunosuppression (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Morbid obesity (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Pregnancy (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
  • Severe mental illness (COVID-19 2022 to 2023)
