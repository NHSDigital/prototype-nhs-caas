Summary

NHS England commissioned Cohorting as a Service (CaaS) to identify people that were eligible for the 2022 to 2023 winter flu vaccination.

To be eligible, they needed to be:

  • 6 months of age or older
  • in defined clinical risk groups shown in the Green Book, chapter 19

CaaS developed specific rules based on the conditions in the Green Book and used medical data to identify people who met those rules.

The data collected helped to:

  • contact or remind people to receive their flu vaccination between 1 September 2022 and 31 March 2023
  • focus protection on those at clinical risk of flu
  • monitor the number of people who received the flu vaccination

Clinical at-risk groups

The links below contain detailed information on the rule logic used to determine who is included and excluded from the digital cohort.

The list is not exhaustive, and decisions should be based on clinical judgement.

The clinical at-risk groups are:

  • Chronic heart disease (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic kidney disease (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic liver disease (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic neurological disease (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Chronic respiratory disease (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Clinically coded eligibility (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Diabetes and adrenal insufficiency (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Immunosuppression including asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Morbid obesity (class 3 obesity) (flu 2022 to 2023)
  • Pregnancy (flu 2022 to 2023)
