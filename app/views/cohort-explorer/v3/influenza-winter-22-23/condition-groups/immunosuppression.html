Influenza vaccinations 2022 to 2023

Immunosuppression including asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen

CaaS digital pathway rule logic

The table below shows the digital cohort rules to determine who should be contacted or reminded to receive their flu vaccination based on specific medical conditions.

These conditions are detailed in influenza Green Book, chapter 19.

Condition description Ruleset Datasets used
Condition description

People with immunosuppression due to disease such as:

  • immunosuppression
  • bone marrow transplant
  • HIV infection at all stages
  • multiple myeloma
  • genetic disorders affecting the immune system (for example, IRAK-4, NEMO, complement disorder)

People with asplenia or dysfunction of the spleen including conditions such as:

  • homozygous sickle cell disease
  • hereditary spherocytosis
  • thalassemia major
  • coeliac syndrome that may lead to splenic dysfunction

It’s difficult to define at what level of immunosuppression a patient could be considered to be at a greater risk of the serious consequences of flu and should be offered a vaccination.

This decision is best made on an individual basis.

Clinicians should use their clinical judgement to inform their decision.

 Ruleset

People who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with immunosuppression (persisting) and not recovered since

And who are also:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with immunosuppression (expiring) within the last 12 months and not recovered since

And people who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with immunosuppression procedure
 Datasets used GPES v4 and GDPPR
Condition description

People with immunosuppression due to treatment such as:

  • people undergoing chemotherapy
  • people treated with or likely to be treated with systemic steroids for more than a month at a dose equivalent to prednisolone at 20mg or more per day (any age)
  • children under 20kg, with a dose of 1mg or more per kg per day
 Ruleset

People who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with immunosuppression drugs in the last 12 months
 Datasets used GPES v4 and GDPPR

Conditions that may not be included in the CaaS digital pathway

If a person receives care from another healthcare provider, for example a hospital, specialist, or community clinic, their GP record is not automatically updated.

If a person’s GP record is not up-to-date when creating a cohort, they may not be included in the digital pathway.

This may result in a person not receiving the invite for their vaccination.

If you believe you're eligible for a vaccination, but have not received your invite, then visit the NHS website on the flu vaccine for more information.

 Condition information

Data about medication prescribed in hospitals and systemic steroid prescriptions in the community is not centrally stored in a way that allows us to determine treatment duration and dose.

If a person is taking systemic steroids prescribed by a GP or hospital-prescribed immunosuppressants, they might not be part of the CaaS digital pathway unless a GP practice indicates their eligibility for flu vaccination using a SNOMED CT code.

Patient data held in GP records generally does not identify people on chemotherapy.

People who are currently or have recently received chemotherapy and are immunosuppressed might not be part of the CaaS digital pathway unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT code to indicate their eligibility for flu vaccination.
