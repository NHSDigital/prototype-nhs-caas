Flu vaccinations 2022 to 2023

Chronic respiratory disease (flu 2022 to 2023)

CaaS digital pathway rule logic

The table below shows the digital cohort rules to determine who should be contacted or reminded to receive their flu vaccination based on specific medical conditions.

These conditions are detailed in influenza Green Book, chapter 19.

Condition description Ruleset Datasets used
Condition description

Asthma that requires continuous or repeated use of inhaled or systemic steroids.

 Ruleset

People who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with asthma diagnosis and not recovered since

And people who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with asthma inhaled corticosteroids within the last 12 months
 Datasets used GPES v4 and GDPPR
Condition description

Asthma with previous exacerbations requiring hospital admission.

 Ruleset

People who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with asthma emergency admissions between 1 September 202 and 31 March 2023
 Datasets used GPES v4 and GDPPR
Condition description

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) including:

  • chronic bronchitis and emphysema
  • bronchiectasis
  • interstitial lung fibrosis
  • pneumoconiosis
  • bronchopulmonary dysplasia (BPD)
 Ruleset

People who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with chronic respiratory disease
 Datasets used GPES v4 and GDPPR

Conditions that may not be included in the CaaS digital pathway

If a person receives care from another healthcare provider, for example a hospital, specialist, or community clinic, their GP record is not automatically updated.

If a person’s GP record is not up-to-date when creating a cohort, they may not be included in the digital pathway.

This may result in a person not receiving the invite for their vaccination.

If you believe you're eligible for a vaccination, but have not received your invite, then visit the NHS website on the flu vaccine for more information.

Condition description Ruleset
Condition description

Asthma that requires continuous or repeated use of inhaled steroids or systemic steroids.

 Condition information

The prescribing data for systemic steroids is not maintained centrally on GP records, so it’s not possible to determine the duration and dose of treatment.

People identified as being on asthma treatment with systemic steroids, may not be included in the CaaS digital pathway unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT administrative code to show eligibility for flu vaccination.
Condition description

Asthma with previous exacerbations requiring hospital admission.

 Condition information

The data held in GP records about a person’s hospital admission is not always complete.

People identified based only on hospital admission data, may not be included in the CaaS digital pathway unless a GP practice uses a SNOMED CT code to show eligibility for flu vaccination.

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) including:

  • chronic bronchitis and emphysema
  • bronchiectasis
  • interstitial lung fibrosis
  • pneumoconiosis
  • bronchopulmonary dysplasia (BPD)
 Condition information

All conditions are included in the CaaS digital pathway
