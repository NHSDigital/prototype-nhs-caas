Influenza vaccinations 2022 to 2023

Chronic neurological disease

CaaS digital pathway rule logic

The table below shows the digital cohort rules to determine who should be contacted or reminded to receive their flu vaccination based on specific medical conditions.

These conditions are detailed in influenza Green Book, chapter 19.

Condition description Ruleset Datasets used
Condition description

Conditions in which respiratory function may be compromised due to neurological disease (e.g. polio syndrome sufferers).

Clinicians should offer immunisation, based on individual assessment, to clinically vulnerable individuals.

This includes those with:

  • cerebral palsy
  • learning disabilities
  • multiple sclerosis and related or similar conditions
  • hereditary and degenerative disease of the nervous system or muscles
  • severe neurological disability

Stroke, transient ischaemic attack (TIA).

 Ruleset

People with:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with chronic neurological disease

And also people who are:

  • over 6 months of age
  • recorded with a learning disability
 Datasets used GPES v4 and GDPPR

Conditions that may not be included in the CaaS digital pathway

If a person receives care from another healthcare provider, for example a hospital, specialist, or community clinic, the information stored in those local computer systems does not automatically update the records of their GP.

The GP record will need to be updated manually using any communication received from other healthcare providers.

This means that some people identified in the clinical policy may not have been included in the digital pathway.

If you believe you're eligible for a vaccination, but have not received your invite, then visit the NHS website on the flu vaccine for more information.
